Portfolio

Designing a personal website.

Year: 2015

Challenge

Designing a portfolio feels both in intensely personal, but insists on being approached objectively from a UX perspective, as well. My goal was to create something quite usable based on interviews and principles discovered throughout the course, and to reintroduce some of the coding skills I’ve developed in the last year.

Approach

I saw this as a chance to start amalgamating my favorite tools and using them in a personal, highly motivated project. I wanted to find a balance between simplicity and design. To that end, the comparative analysis was incredibly useful. I performed an overview of several portfolios, and eventually distilled my findings into implementable takeaways.

Process

First, I began interviewing professionals about what HR managers are like in general. I found that they are often young, and borrow from a variety of sources when it comes to making hiring decisions. They are not always the most tech-savvy people in the organization, but are dedicated to finding fits for positions that make money for and meet company objectives.

Second, I started sketching and wire framing design ideas using pen/paper and Sketch 3, a new favorite tool. I read that one-page scrolling websites are actually effective at getting stories across, and because a portfolio is primarily a storytelling medium, chose that option. I realized, though, that organizing projects demanded adding other pages because of the depth with which I wanted to explore each.

Finally, I chose Zurb’s Foundation as my framework because of its responsiveness and familiarity. Using those tools and plugins, I built the site.
